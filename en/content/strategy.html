Strategy

AWG 2016 – more than a sporting event
 
In 2002, Nuuk and the rest of Greenland attempted for the first time to host the Arctic Winter Games. Nuuk and Iqaluit co-hosted the event, which not only was a resounding success for the whole of Greenland but an unforgettable experience that left a lasting sense of pride among the population.
In 2016, Greenland will once again host the Arctic Winter Games. This time, Greenland – Nuuk – will be sole hosts of the games, the biggest single event in Greenland’s history. Approximately twice the size of the games in 2002, the project will culminate in a week’s worth of sporting and cultural events with about 2,000 visitors in March 2016. However, the project is already underway and extends far beyond a week of sporting and cultural celebration. Building on experience from 2002, it is a dynamic project that must develop societal skills, be inclusive and ensure that we live up to our host role in 2016.
Just as in 2002, AWG 2016 must be an event that generates pride and invaluable experience. The core AWG values of joy, energy and community must be given a Greenlandic slant, drawing on everything that is unique to the region. This strategy explains our goals for AWG 2016 and how we envisage achieving these established goals. The AWG 2016 vision, mission and values form the strategy foundation.
 

 
“The Arctic Winter Games is a high profile circumpolar sport competition for northern and Arctic athletes.  The Games provide an opportunity to strengthen sport development in the participants’ jurisdictions, to promote the benefits of sport, to build partnerships, and to promote culture and values.  The Games celebrate sport, social exchange and cultures. The Games provide an opportunity for the developing athlete to compete in friendly competition while sharing cultural values from northern regions around the world.”
 
www.arcticwintergames.org
 
 

 
 


Vision

  • AWG 2016 unites Greenland around a central communal event celebrating sporting and cultural enjoyment.
  • AWG 2016 generates pride and joy while developing social voluntariness and societal skills.
  • AWG 2016 focuses global attention on Greenland, strengthening ties with other Arctic nations.

 
Mission
To ensure the success of Greenland’s biggest sporting and cultural event through diverse cooperation across sectors, organisations and businesses.
To include as many as possible in the organisation of AWG 2016 and developing people’s skills through courses and practical AWG 2016 tasks.
To highlight and represent Greenland in the best way possible through close collaboration between the business sector, the public sector and the general population.
To support and raise awareness of sport, health and culture among Arctic youth in particular. 

Values
Community
To partake in diverse, inclusive and colourful community activities across society and the Arctic region, where everyone can be part of a meaningful project that generates joy.
Curiosity
We are part of the Arctic community and are curious about each other’s similarities, differences and what we can learn from each other. 
Courage
Through sport, culture and community we find the strength and courage to develop and set new goals. 
 
 



COMMUNITY
 
Involving all of Greenland
While the Municipality of Sermersooq and the city of Nuuk are hosting AWG 2016, all of Greenland is responsible and must take credit for holding and providing the framework for AWG 2016. Involvement in AWG2016 must therefore not be restricted to municipal and/or city boundaries. To ensure a more unified project and games, we must identify simple effective means of spreading the project across the whole country so that everyone in Greenland is encouraged to play an active role in AWG 2016. The project must be developed in such a way that it benefits all of Greenland’s different regions. Sportsmen and women and cultural profiles from different parts of the country must become actively involved in the holding of the games – just as the AWG 2016 secretariat will tour the country to establish collaborative projects with the large municipalities, educational institutions, voluntary organisations and sports associations.
Greenland’s strengths and diversity must be visually exploited in AWG 2016 marketing. While experiencing a degree of recognisability, both Greenland’s population and people from other AWG countries will also learn something new and unique through AWG 2016.
Specific initiatives:
·         Prior to AWG 2016, we will visit the five biggest towns in Greenland and Kommuneqarfik Sermersooq’s four towns (Nuuk, Tasiilaq, Ittoqqortoormiit, Paamiut, Sisimiut, Ilulissat, Aasiaat, Qaqortoq).
·         Twenty volunteers from other towns than Nuuk will attend project management courses and act as AWG 2016 volunteers. Ten of these will be students from institutions of study outside Nuuk.
·         Youth competitions will be held in different parts of the country so young people can travel to Nuuk and experience AWG 2016.
·         Cultural profiles from all regions will participate in gala shows and other cultural events during AWG 2016.
 
 
 
Cross-sectoral collaboration
AWG 2016 is to be promoted as the communal project it is and one which can only succeed if all areas of society work together. A project that will benefit sport, business, culture and Greenland in general. The project must identify new ways for businesses, volunteers, the public sector and society to work together to generate positive results. This is extremely important knowledge for a small society like Greenland where collaboration and cooperation are crucial to societal development.
There is a great deal of useful experience to draw on from the other AWG countries, AWGIC and those previously involved with AWG. Those willing to help on the basis of their experience will be involved in planning AWG2016.
Specific initiatives:
·         We will organise practical planning and concept development seminars across sectors and businesses.
·         We will establish an interdisciplinary task force that will address special problems requiring various experts working in unison to find viable project solutions.
 
 
 

CURIOSITY
 
Promoting Greenland
Promoting AWG 2016 must be linked to a larger, forward-looking and positive presentation of Greenland. While promotional initiatives will include elements from Visit Greenland’s brand to provide cohesion, the story will nonetheless focus on the uniqueness of AWG 2016. Experience of marketing in Greenland must be used to create a professional and convincing presentation of AWG within the Greenlandic context. Similarly, the tourist sector must incorporate AWG into its marketing of Greenland as an interesting tourist destination – as is the case with sporting events such as the Arctic Circle Race, for example.
AWG 2016 cannot succeed without collaboration and partnerships with people, organisations and businesses at home and abroad. Sponsors are extremely important project partners due to their economic and professional support and the signal value of their involvement in AWG 2016. Sponsors must be recognised for their support and AWG 2016 will use different platforms and opportunities to promote them and create a positive holistic story that includes them.
AWG 2016 will take place in Nuuk, which provides a unique opportunity to showcase everything the town has to offer with all its contrasts and uniqueness. Providing the unforgettable encounter with Nuuk is a task that will fall to the citizens of the city.
Specific initiatives:
·         Make AWG 2016 and thus Greenland interesting to relevant international media through good website information, stories in different media, professional press relations etc.
·         Have clear marketing standards for different sponsors so that they are clear as to what they are getting.
·         During AWG 2016 there will be a VIP lounge for key sponsors and guests where the press can also meet with them.
·         Prepare citizens to act as hosts through skills development and good communication about AWG 2016.
·         Cooperation with Visit Greenland on their Urban Greenland concept.
 
Knowledge about the Arctic
AWG is built up around the Arctic community and living and residing in the Arctic regions. It is therefore vital that the general level of knowledge about the Arctic and specifically about AWG participating countries is anchored in AWG 2016 participants and the citizens of Greenland. Leading up to AWG 2016 and during the games, we will therefore focus on the Arctic countries at the different events and campaigns.
Specific initiatives:
·         Information material on the Arctic and AWG will be distributed to all state schools in Greenland prior to the commencement of the games in 2016, just as a survey will be carried out to test children’s awareness of both topics before and after the games.
·         In collaboration with institutions in Nuuk, cultural events informing about countries and regions/territories participating in AWG and the Arctic union will be held before and during AWG 2016.
·         General informative material on the Arctic will also be produced and distributed.
 
 

 
COURAGE
 
Skills development
An important aim of AWG 2016 is to promote skills development in the general population, giving us the strength and courage to develop and set new goals.  Skills development will be rooted in project work, event coordination, language, service and not least the hosting role developed through active participation and courses. Skills development will also take place at different levels and have a varying content depending on people’s skills level, needs and affiliation with AWG 2016 – e.g. volunteers, business employees, students and those working in the service industry. To achieve this aim, AWG has entered into sponsorships focusing on skills development and we will also create an e-learning platform supporting skills development. AWG also actively supports strengthening voluntariness in general in Greenland – a goal which it pursues, in part, through strong ties with the Greenland Sports Federation (GIF) and Meeqqat Inuunerissut (MIBB).
Greenland must ensure that future generations can draw on the lessons learned from AWG 2016, which is why documenting the project work, decision processes, planning and practical implementation will be an important task for the AWG 2016 secretariat.
 
Specific initiatives:
·         Project work courses for about 300 people.
·         Service courses for about 150 people.
·         First-aid courses for about 150 people.
·         English courses for about 50 people.
·         In five towns, courses in volunteer work will be held in collaboration with the Greenland Sports Federation.
·         Preparations for AWG 2016 will be documented through TV programmes produced in collaboration with KNR.
·         Development of a database and concept for volunteers.
 
 
 
Sport and culture
Sport and culture lie at the heart of AWG. Spearheaded by youth, participants explore and challenge their own abilities in a spirit of mutual inspiration. The AWG 2016 framework must benefit participants, and prior to the holding of the games, we must establish a connection that creates a sense of joyful anticipation in the minds of athletes taking part in AWG 2016.
AWG 2016 creates new opportunities for young people to participate in sport, improving their health and quality of life. Together with GIF in particular, AWG 2016 supports a more long-term development of conditions for athletes – among other things by creating new sports centres, improving sports facilities and introducing new sports to Greenland.
Greenland’s artists must be able to use AWG 2016 to develop their skills and try out new art forms or forms of expression. During AWG 2016, they must highlight our rich cultural diversity in an innovative way. Together with artists from other AWG countries, they must act as ambassadors of Arctic culture, entertain and leave a lasting impression.

  • Through digital platforms, we will establish contact with young athletes and artists prior to AWG 2016.
  • AWG 2016 will organise events focusing on health and exercise.
  • We will assess young people’s motivation for exercise, healthy lifestyle and societal involvement before and after AWG 2016.
  • Together with GIF, we will work to ensure the participation of Greenland’s athletes in all sport disciplines at AWG2016, including the ‘new’ sports in Greenland: biathlon, basketball, wrestling and snowshoeing.
  • Artists from all regions of the country must be actively involved in the games.
